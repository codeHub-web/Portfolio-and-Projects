Interview Tips
1. Prepare for Common Interview Questions:

Anticipate questions like "Tell me about yourself" or "What are your strengths and weaknesses?" and have clear, concise answers.

Tell me about a time when you had to work with a difficult colleague?

Give me an example of a time you had to prioritize multiple tasks. How did you manage your time?


2. Do Your Research:

Learn about the company’s culture, values, and products. Understanding the company will help you answer questions more effectively.

Learning about your potential employer not only shows your interest in the company and your attention to detail, but helps innderstanding a company's culture not only helps you assess whether it’s a good fit for you but also prepares you to succeed in the role and build a meaningful, long-term career


3. Practice Behavioral Questions:

Expect behavioral questions like "Tell me about a time you faced a challenge." Use the STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) method to frame your answers.
